Reduce food waste

THE FOOD WASTE CRISIS?

Approximately one third of all food in Romania ends up in the trash
or is unnecessarily wasted annually. This amount corresponds to about
2.55 million tons of food products. Unfortunately, waste can be found at all
levels of society, from the smallest household to the largest enterprise.

Learn more

HOW DOES IT AFFECT US?

The discarded food combines with the already existing waste and begins
a slow process of decomposition, which releases methane. Methane is one
of the most powerful greenhouse gases, 28x stronger than carbon dioxide.
Once in the atmosphere, it contributes massively to global warming.
Each product that arrives in front of you has gone through countless
stages such as collection, packaging or transport, thus involving the
consumption of several resources, the best example being the fuel
used for transport and other machinery which as we very well know is one of the great
causes of pollution.

Learn more

IMPACT ON THE ENVIRONMENT:

By producing and consuming as much as is needed and no more, we save resources.
Food waste also means water waste: a burger needs 2400 liters of water to produce.
If we waste even a fraction of it, we have also wasted the said water.
Having said that, why sabotage ourselves when we can live better and longer?
